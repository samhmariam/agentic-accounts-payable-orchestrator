# IDENTITY EXCEPTION REQUEST + RESPONSE

## Purpose

Demonstrate the complete request-and-response cycle for an identity architecture
exception, practising the enterprise governance process under constrained conditions.

## Required Headings

### Part A — Exception Request

1. Exception ID and date
2. Requestor role
3. Requested exception — what normal control is being bypassed and why
4. Business justification — time-bounded, specific, non-speculative
5. Risk acceptance — what risk the requestor is accepting
6. Compensating controls — what is in place to reduce the risk while the exception is active
7. Expiry — when the exception lapses and what must happen at expiry
8. Approver target — who the request is addressed to

### Part B — Approver Response

1. Decision — approved / rejected / approved with conditions
2. Conditions — if approved with conditions, what they are
3. Duration — exact expiry date (not "for now")
4. Post-approval obligations — what the requestor must do after approval
5. Review date — when the approver or designee will review the exception again

## Guiding Questions

- What would cause this request to be rejected immediately?
- Who besides the primary approver should be informed of this exception?
- What observable signal would trigger early revocation before the expiry date?
- What audit evidence must exist one year from now to prove this exception was legitimate?

## Acceptance Criteria

- Both Part A and Part B are completed (request without response is incomplete)
- Compensating controls are specific — not "monitoring will be in place"
- Duration in Part B is a date — not "temporary" or "until resolved"
- Post-approval obligations include at least one verification step
- Review date is within 90 days of approval date
