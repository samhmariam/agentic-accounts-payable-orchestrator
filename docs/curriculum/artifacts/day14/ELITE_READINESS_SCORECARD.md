# ELITE READINESS SCORECARD

<!-- CAPSTONE_B: This artifact applies to both AP and claims intake transfer domain -->

## Purpose

Trainee self-assessment of elite FDE readiness across the eight mental models
and five rubric dimensions, demonstrating metacognitive awareness of growth
across the 14 days.

## Required Headings

1. Mental model mastery — for each of the eight models: one evidence example from a specific day and one remaining growth area
2. Rubric dimension self-score — for each of the five dimensions: score (0–35 or 0–20 or 0–15), one piece of evidence, one improvement to make
3. Transfer domain reflection — how applying patterns to claims intake changed your understanding of the original AP design
4. One decision you would make differently — a specific technical or process decision, why, and what evidence changed your view
5. Remaining development plan — what you will focus on in the first 90 days as an FDE

## Guiding Questions

- Which mental model changed a decision you had already made?
- Which dimension did you score yourself most generously and is that honest?
- What surprised you most about the claims intake transfer domain?
- If you were assessing someone else on Day 14, what would you most want to see in their defense?

## Structural Example — Evidence Line

Use short, evidence-backed entries rather than generic reflection:

- `Authority is not provenance` — Day 11 changed my integration design because I stopped trusting queue payload identity and required OBO-bound actor verification before write-path approval.
- `Operational elegance is reversible evidence` — Day 14 changed my rollout posture because canary promotion without rollback readiness is not operational excellence, it is optimism.

## Honesty Guardrail

- A self-score without a cited day, artifact, or gate is incomplete.
- "I improved a lot" is not evidence.
- If a dimension is weak, say so plainly and name the next concrete practice step.

## Acceptance Criteria

- All 8 mental models addressed with a specific day reference (not generic statements)
- All 5 rubric dimensions self-scored with evidence
- Transfer domain reflection is specific — not "it was similar but different"
- One decision changed entry names the specific decision and the evidence that changed it
- Development plan is actionable — not aspirational ("I will practise X by doing Y within Z weeks")
