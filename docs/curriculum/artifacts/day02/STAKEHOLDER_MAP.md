# STAKEHOLDER MAP

## Purpose

Identify every stakeholder with power over or accountability for the AegisAP
system, their concerns, and their relationship to the product.

## Required Headings

1. Stakeholder inventory table (role, organisation unit, stance, primary concern)
2. Power / interest mapping (2×2: high–low power × high–low interest)
3. Conflict pairs — stakeholders whose primary concerns are in tension
4. Communication strategy per quadrant
5. Key missing stakeholder risk — who is absent from the map and what could go wrong

## Guiding Questions

- Which stakeholder has veto power over a production deployment and what would trigger their veto?
- Where does the "security vs velocity" tension live in this map?
- Who owns the system of record for invoice data — is it the same person who owns the agent's output?
- If the agent makes a wrong payment, which stakeholder gets the phone call first?

## Acceptance Criteria

- Minimum 6 distinct roles mapped (not personas — real enterprise roles)
- Conflict pairs section names the conflict, not just the people
- Power/interest quadrants are used to inform the communication strategy
- At least one missing stakeholder identified with a specific risk scenario
- Role names are enterprise-grade (e.g., "Finance Controller", not "finance person")
